About

"I am currently a faculty member and the Fulton entrepreneurial postdoc associate at the ASU Active Perception Group (APG) . I closely work with Professor Dr. Yezhou Yang within the School of Computing and Augmented Intelligence (SCAI) at Arizona State University (ASU) in Tempe."

At the core of my academic pursuits lies a steadfast commitment to advancing the field of Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS), with a focus on the intricate domains of traffic intersection monitoring and safety. My research narrative is underscored by the intricate synergy between cutting-edge neuromorphic vision-based (Event-based) systems and the transformative power of AI. This symbiotic fusion enables me to confront and surmount the multifaceted challenges that define contemporary transportation infrastructure.
Beyond the confines of transportation systems, my scholarly curiosity extends into the domain of motion capture (MoCAP) technology, where I explore the nuanced intricacies of human activity sensing, reconstruction, recognition, and visual analysis (Open projects â€“ Hand gestures based 3D interactions in Virtual Reality).
